# Learned

## Product Context

- Fairplay is a mobile-first household responsibility planning tool for two default personas, Alex and Max.
- The product should make responsibility ownership, hidden coordination, radar topics, and check-in decisions clear without partner scoring or blame.
- Shared household credentials mean persona-private drafts are UX-filtered records, not true secrecy from someone who knows the household password.
- PWA work should support installability and responsive UI, but v1 should not cache sensitive household data offline.

## Visual Constraints

- Cute character support is allowed when it stays warm, adult, and practical.
- Responsibility summaries may be compact operational panels, but should not mimic physical cards, card decks, printable layouts, workbook grids, source boards, or Trello-like columns.
- Radar visuals should imply shared attention and gentle review, not alarm, proof, or accusation.
- Check-in completion should feel calm and relieving, not like a trophy, grade, or compliance reward.

## T09 Dependency

T09 must not invent character art on its own. It should use this task's handoff and approved assets as the source of visual direction, then integrate them only after core screens exist.

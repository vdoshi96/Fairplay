# Book Source Notes

The redesign uses `docs/research/fair-play-book-report.md` and `docs/research/a-better-share-book-report.md` as conceptual inputs. UI copy is paraphrased and original.

## Concepts Reflected

- Visible work versus hidden work: the opening lesson now separates observable work from planning, remembering, coordination, follow-up, and care.
- Owner versus helper: the course distinguishes accountable ownership from completing a step or serving as backup.
- CPE: the CPE lesson names conception, planning, and execution as the lifecycle of ownership.
- Good enough standards: the standard lesson keeps the focus on shared, realistic household expectations and owner autonomy.
- Physical, cognitive, and emotional load: the course names these load dimensions in the opening and dynamic fairness lessons.
- Treadmill versus finite tasks: the board-lanes lesson explains why recurring work can carry a different load profile than one-time projects.
- Handoffs and training: the handoff lesson now includes context transfer, practice, and review.
- Radar and check-in rituals: the radar lesson turns signals into decisions, acknowledgement, deferral, repair, or review dates.
- Dynamic fairness: the fairness lesson avoids 50/50 scoring and points to season, capacity, health, travel, children, recovery, and load mix.
- Appreciation, repair, deferral, and safety boundaries: the repair lesson supports private drafting, pausing, outside support, and not forcing unsafe check-ins.

## Language Guardrails

- Kept CPE because it is already accepted project language.
- Avoided branded source acronyms and book-specific scripts.
- Avoided labels that diagnose or shame either partner.
- Used "good enough" rather than source-specific standard names in UI copy.
